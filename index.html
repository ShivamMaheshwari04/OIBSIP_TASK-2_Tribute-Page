A.P.J Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

About the Legend


A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, whose full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.Kalam's journey in the field of science and technology began when he earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology, and in 1958, he joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). In 1969, he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he became the project director of the SLV-III, which was India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle.His contribution to the development of successful missiles earned him the nickname "Missile Man," and one of the significant achievements during his tenure was the launch of Agni, India's first intermediate-range ballistic missile, in 1989.

Kalam played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power. Although these tests raised international concerns, they also solidified Kalam's status as a national hero.Beyond his technical achievements, Kalam was a visionary leader. In 1998, he presented a comprehensive plan called Technology Vision 2020, outlining the path to transform India into a developed society within two decades. The plan emphasized technology, increased agricultural productivity, and improved access to healthcare and education.

Throughout his illustrious career, Kalam received numerous accolades, including seven honorary doctorates from 40 universities. He was honored by the Government of India with the Padma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 for his outstanding work with ISRO and DRDO.In 1997, Kalam was bestowed with India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, in recognition of his significant contributions to scientific research and the modernization of defense technology in the country. Additionally, he received the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society in 2013, acknowledging his exceptional management and leadership in space-related projects.Tragically, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong on July 27, 2015, Kalam suffered an apparent cardiac arrest and passed away at the age of 83.To commemorate his legacy, Wheeler Island, a national missile test site in Odisha, was renamed Kalam Island in September 2015. Furthermore, a prominent road in New Delhi was renamed from Aurangzeb Road to Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Road in August 2015, and in February 2018, scientists from the Botanical Survey of India honored him by naming a newly found plant species as Drypetes kalamii.

Achievements


1. Missile Man of India: Known as the "Missile Man of India," A.P.J. Abdul Kalam played a pivotal role in developing India's indigenous missile technology.

2. President of India: Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, making him the first scientist and bachelor to hold this esteemed office.

3. Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests: He played a significant role in the successful execution of the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which bolstered India's nuclear capabilities.

4. Agni and Prithvi Missiles: Under his leadership, the development of Agni and Prithvi missiles was initiated, making India self-reliant in defense technology.

5. Bharat Ratna: In 1997, A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was honored with India's highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna, for his exceptional contributions to science and technology.

6. Abdul Kalam Island: The Wheeler Island, a missile testing facility off the coast of Odisha, was renamed as Abdul Kalam Island in 2015 as a tribute to his contributions.

7. Inspiring Youth: Dr. Kalam had an unwavering dedication to inspiring and motivating the youth of India, encouraging them to pursue careers in science and innovation.

8. Ignited Minds: He authored the book "Ignited Minds," emphasizing the importance of unleashing the potential of the youth to build a prosperous nation.

9. People's President: During his presidency, he became widely known as the "People's President" due to his approachable and down-to-earth demeanor.

10. Vision 2020: Dr. Kalam had a vision for India to become a developed nation by the year 2020, and he worked tirelessly to promote economic and technological advancements to achieve this goal.

11. Providing Access to Rural Areas: He was an advocate for providing access to technology and education in rural areas, aiming to bridge the digital divide.

12. Research and Education: Even after his presidency, he continued to contribute to research and education by teaching and interacting with students in various institutes.

13. Wings of Fire: Dr. Kalam's autobiography, "Wings of Fire," is an inspiring account of his life journey from humble beginnings to becoming a renowned scientist and statesman.

14. Rashtrapati Bhavan Open to Public: As President, he initiated the practice of opening up the Rashtrapati Bhavan to the public, making it more accessible and transparent.

15. Humble and Simple: Despite his numerous accolades, Dr. Abdul Kalam remained humble and lived a simple life, serving as an inspiration to people across the world.
